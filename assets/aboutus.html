Facts Related To Blood Donation

1. The approximate distribution of blood types in world population is given below:
O-positive: 38 percent
O-negative: 7 percent
A-positive: 34 percent
A-negative: 6 percent
B-positive: 9 percent
B-negative: 2 percent
AB-positive: 3 percent
AB-negative: 1 percent
2 Type O-negative blood is called the universal donor type because it is compatible with any blood type.
3. Type AB-positive blood is called the universal recipient type because a person who has it can receive blood of any type.
4. One donation can help to save the lives of three people.
5. If you began donating blood at the age of 18 and donated every 90 days until you reached 60,you would have donated 30 gallons of blood,potentially helping to save more than 500 lives!
6. Donors can give either whole blood or spcific blood components(RBC,WBC & plasma) only.
The process of donating specific blood components is called apheresis.

Health Benefits Of Donating Blood

Blood donation not only makes the receiver’s life good but also helps the donor to maintain good health.
The health benefits of donating blood are mentioned below.
1.PREVENTS HEMOCHROMATOSIS:-
Hemochromatosis is a health condition that arises due to excess absorption of iron by the body.
Regular blood donation may help in reducing iron overload. A reduction in the iron level in the body is linked with low cancer risk.
2.WEIGHT LOSS:-
Regular blood donation reduces the weight of the donors. This is helpful to those who are obese and are at higher risk of
cardiovascular diseases and other health disorders.
3.STIMULATES BLOOD CELL PRODUCTION:-
After donating blood, the body works to replenish the blood loss. This stimulates the production of new blood cells and in turn,
helps in maintaining good health.
